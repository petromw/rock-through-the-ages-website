These are a few of the most influential and popular artists and bands in rock

Bob Dylan

Bob Dylan was an essential influence in the popularization of rock music. Throughout the 60s, Dylan's was the voice of revolution. His music called for change in society, which was enticing to the youth and quickly gained a strong following. His music had a folk sound and relied heavily on the lyrics. His songs have likely been remade and covered more than any other artists ever. It would be hard to find someone that has never heard a song by Bob Dylan, or at least a cover by another popular artist, such as Jimi Hendrix or Guns N' Roses.

Jimi Hendrix

Jimi Hendrix is arguable one of the most influential artists in rock history. He is also regarded by many to be one of the most talented guitarists of all time. He demonstrated the extent to which a guitar can be used as an instrument, which lead to experimentation by other artist and developments in musical style. His music relied heavily on distorted guitar and instrumental sounds. He was very experimental with his sound and while his career was very short he was, and still is, one of the most popular rock artists of all time.

The Beatles

The Beatles were made up of four members: John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison and Ringo Starr. Together they made up one of the most well known bands to have ever produced music. Throughout their career, they recorded a couple hundred songs, and sold almost two hundred million albums. They were extremely successful both commercially and artistically. The Beatles have a huge variety of styles of music because they were constantly experimenting with new sounds. Their sounds vary from old rock and roll to very psychedelic sounds.

Pink Floyd

Pink Floyd is a band with a fascinating history. In the band's early days, the original singer and front-man, Syd Barrett, started the bands trend of experimental, psychedelic music and cohesive concept albums. For each album, Pink Floyd viewed them as a project. Almost all of their albums were concept albums, which means that they have a message, theme, or story throughout all of the songs on the album. Their albums were also linked by musical motifs which makes them easy to follow and enjoyable to listen to all the way through. Pink Floyd experimented with a lot of sounds and each album has it's own unique style. The tone of each album is consistent with the message they were trying to convey.

Bruce Springsteen

Bruce Springsteen, aka "The Boss", was a huge success with audiences of all backgrounds, although he cared most about the American working class. Springsteen grew up in a working class family with limited income, and he did not have a good relationship with his father. His childhood had a huge influence on his music. Springsteen is a both story teller and a musician at heart. He took these two talents and developed his style of music, which is, at it's core, stories set to music. Springsteen's sound is influenced by rock and roll and folk rock. He has a distinct deep gravelly voice and a powerful rock sound. His music is complimented by his band, but most importantly Clarence Clemons who was his saxophonist and good friend.

Led Zeppelin

Led Zeppelin is the sound that most people think of when they hear 'classic rock'. They have a blues influenced sound but it is much more electric and fast. They rely heavily on guitars and drums. Led Zeppelin has some of the most recognizable songs ever written. They were a huge part of the classic rock movement and still have a vast following to this day even though they no longer make music. Zeppelin's songs are distinguishable by long guitar solos and heavy lyrics.
